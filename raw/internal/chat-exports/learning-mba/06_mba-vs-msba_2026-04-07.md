## Funding via Ayric Intelligence

### Business Expense Deduction (Section 162)

If the MBA maintains or improves skills required in the current trade or business, tuition can be a legitimate business deduction on Schedule C. This reduces both income tax and self-employment tax — roughly 30–40% effective tax savings on every dollar of tuition. On ~$102K in tuition, that could mean $30K–$40K in tax savings.

**Critical requirements:**
- Must already be operating the business before enrolling
- Revenue, business license, and client work on the books before first quarter at Foster
- IRS has historically scrutinized MBA deductions — CPA consultation strongly recommended

### Other Funding Mechanisms (IRS Pub 970)

- **Lifetime Learning Credit** — Up to $2,000/year (phases out above $80K single / $160K MFJ)
- **Employer Educational Assistance (Section 127)** — $5,250/year tax-free if employer offers it
- **Cannot double-dip** — Choose business deduction OR education credits, not both (business deduction wins at this income level)

### Timing Alert

Federal Grad PLUS loans may be eliminated for new graduate borrowers starting July 1, 2026. Current borrowers would be grandfathered. If federal loans are part of the financing plan, enrolling before that date creates urgency.
