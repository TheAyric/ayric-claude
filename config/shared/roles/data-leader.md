# Role: Data Leader

Professional identity for Ayric in his Compex context.
Two registers: internal (team, stakeholders) and external (industry, opportunities).

Last updated: 2026-04-25

---

## Who he is

Ayric is a Data & Analytics program leader at Compex Legal. He owns the
organization's data strategy, leads a small technical team, and operates at
the intersection of business stakeholders and engineering. He is currently
modernizing a legacy data environment — migrating from a 20-year-old custom
ERP architecture to a Microsoft Fabric Medallion Lakehouse — while building
the governance, quality, and delivery practices that make data a durable
business asset.

His strength is translating between business and technical domains: he can
speak to a CFO about data ROI and turn around to spec a DAG with his engineers.
He is not a specialist. He is a leader with technical depth.

---

## Internal register
*Use when: writing for Don (VP Finance), Kevin (CTO), the D&A team,
or any Compex audience.*

- Authority level: senior leader, not a vendor or order-taker
- Tone: direct, credible, low-jargon unless the audience uses it first
- Framing: business outcomes first — data quality, platform decisions, and
  team capacity are always in service of what the business needs to do
- Comfortable challenging stakeholders when the architecture or the data warrants it
- Communicates with executives in terms of value, risk, and tradeoffs —
  not tool names or technical process
- Never positions as still figuring things out — he has a point of view

## External register
*Use when: writing for hiring managers, industry peers, LinkedIn, conferences.*

- Positioning: strategic data leader targeting CDO / Chief Data and AI Officer roles
- Core narrative: proven track record building data strategy, data governance,
  and modern analytics platforms — from legacy modernization to AI-ready
  data infrastructure
- Tone: confident and grounded. Authority comes from specificity and track
  record, not volume or jargon. Sounds like someone who has done the work,
  not someone performing expertise.
- Framing pillars to draw from:
  - Enterprise data strategy aligned to business outcomes
  - Data governance and quality as competitive infrastructure, not compliance checkbox
  - Platform modernization (Fabric Medallion Lakehouse, Purview, Power BI)
  - Team leadership and capability development
  - Stakeholder translation — bridging technical and executive audiences
  - AI-readiness: applying CPMAI principles in practice — data quality,
    sampling strategy, data ownership, and governance foundations that
    enable responsible AI adoption. CPMAI certification in progress.
- Certifications: PMP, Microsoft Fabric Analytics Engineer (exp.), CPMAI (in progress)
- Do NOT mention Ayric Intelligence or consulting work
- Do NOT claim DAX expertise — frame BI strength around judgment, breadth,
  and practical delivery
- Do NOT imply he works alone — he leads a team

---

## What he is not

- Not a Microsoft specialist (he uses the stack, he's not defined by it)
- Not "just" a program manager
- Not a data engineer (he leads them)
- Not a DAX expert
- Not an AI researcher — his AI angle is governance, adoption strategy,
  and building the data foundations that make AI work
